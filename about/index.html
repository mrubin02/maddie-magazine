A Letter From the Editor

I started this magazine because I kept having opinions and nowhere particular to put them. That is an embarrassing thing to admit. I am admitting it anyway.

The publications I actually read — the ones I steal from waiting rooms, the ones I look up at 2am because someone mentioned them once — have a particular quality. They take things seriously without being solemn about it. They are funny without being dismissive. They have a voice. A voice that, when you encounter it, makes you feel like you are being spoken to directly and slightly conspiratorially, by someone who has thought about this more than you have but is not going to make that your problem.

I wanted to make something like that. Smaller, obviously. More personal, because everything I make is more personal than it should probably be. Less authoritative, because I am one person with strong opinions and a broadband connection, which is a dangerous combination but also, historically, the condition under which most interesting things get written.

This magazine is about things I find interesting. Which includes: the specific emotional texture of ordinary experiences that nobody has quite named yet; the way we talk about ourselves, to each other and online; cultural phenomena that deserve more scrutiny than they are currently receiving; cultural phenomena that are receiving too much scrutiny; and the occasional piece I cannot justify on any grounds except that I really wanted to write it.

I do not have a thesis. I have a sensibility. I have been told that is enough. I am choosing to believe it.

Welcome to Maddie Magazine. I hope you find something worth keeping.

— Maddie Rubin
